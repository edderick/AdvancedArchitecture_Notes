Logic gates

From Sotonpedia
Jump to: navigation, search

There are many types of logic gates, but only a few core ones that actually matter and you need to know about.
They generally follow the same rules as discrete maths logic, but the difference is that they actually affect the output of the circuit.
All logic gates can also be simulated via NAND gates (or NOR gates if you want). This is very commonly used as it is much cheaper to make many NAND gates then a variety of ANDs, NOTs, ORs etc...
(The only reason that NAND gates are used over NORs is because of choice and convention, it was like the blu-ray vs HD format wars but *much* geekier.)
